The problem we address

Between Canadian auctions and Lagos ports, dealers face opaque pricing, enforcement risk and incomplete information. The result: margin loss, delays and in some cases abandoned imports.

VIN fraud & misrepresentation

Cloned or invalid VINs, salvage histories and incomplete disclosures make it difficult to know what is truly being purchased before payment leaves Nigeria.

Unpredictable landed cost

Duties, levies, port charges and FX movements mean the final cost of each vehicle can diverge sharply from expectations, undermining dealer pricing and planning.

Customs delays & penalties

Missing or inconsistent documentation leads to valuation disputes, storage fees and delayed release at Apapa, Tin Can or Onne.

How the workflow operates

Auto-Bridge links Canadian sellers, VIN data, inspection capacity, logistics providers and Nigeria Customs brokers into a single, traceable process.

1

Vehicle selection & VIN screening

Dealers submit vehicle details or auction IDs. Auto-Bridge runs VIN history checks, screening for theft, salvage and mileage anomalies before bids are placed or payments are approved.

2

Certified inspection

Canada-based inspectors conduct structured photo/video reviews and basic mechanical checks. Reports are shared through the dealer portal for decision making.

3

Landed-cost estimation

Our cost engine combines FOB, freight, duties, levies, port and clearing fees to offer a pre-purchase landed-cost estimate, with variance bands reflecting regulatory and FX conditions.

4

Logistics & documentation

Auto-Bridge coordinates trucking to port, shipping line bookings and Customs documentation via licensed brokers, aligned with e-Customs and AEO processes where available.

5

Tracking & clearance

Dealers track progress from Canada to Lagos via the portal, with notifications for key milestones: inspection complete, vessel sailed, vessel arrived, clearance completed.
